# Jobs to Be Done — Omiigo Car

Jobs exist independent of Omiigo Car. They describe what car owners are trying to accomplish in their lives — whether they use this app, a spreadsheet, or nothing at all.

## How to Read This

Each job follows the JTBD format:

> **When** [situation], **I want to** [motivation], **so I can** [expected outcome].

Jobs are grouped by persona. Struggling moments capture the real-world triggers that create urgency around each job.

---

## Der Alltagsfahrer (Daily Driver)

*Drives daily for commuting and errands. Maintains the car out of necessity, not passion. Checks maintenance when prompted, forgets otherwise.*

### Job 1: Know what my car needs right now

> **When** I'm about to drive somewhere or thinking about my car on the weekend, **I want to** see at a glance if anything is overdue or coming up, **so I can** handle it before it becomes a breakdown or a failed inspection.

This is the #1 job. Car maintenance is invisible until something breaks. Most owners don't have a system — they rely on the oil light or the TÜV sticker on the windshield.

**Struggling moments:**

- Driving to work and suddenly remembering "wasn't the oil change due last month?" but having no way to check
- Getting a TÜV reminder letter and realizing the appointment is in 3 days with no preparation
- The car making a new noise and not knowing when brakes were last checked
- Lending the car to a family member and worrying they'll ignore the warning lights

### Job 2: Record that I did something so I don't lose track

> **When** I've just completed a maintenance task (oil change, tire swap, inspection), **I want to** mark it done and have the next due date calculated automatically, **so I can** stop thinking about it until it's actually due again.

The relief of checking something off is half the value. The other half is not having to remember when it's due next.

**Struggling moments:**

- Changing tires in the garage and thinking "when did I do this last year?" with no record
- Coming back from the mechanic and having no place to note what was done
- Opening the glove box to find 3 old inspection receipts but no clear timeline

### Job 3: Keep my car's identity in one place

> **When** I need to reference my car's details (plate number, VIN, mileage, model year), **I want to** pull them up instantly, **so I can** fill out forms, call the mechanic, or check compatibility without walking to the car.

**Struggling moments:**

- Standing at the parts counter and not knowing the exact model year
- Filing an insurance claim and searching for the VIN
- Calling ADAC and fumbling for the license plate number

## Die Familienverwalterin (Family Fleet Manager)

*Manages 2–3 household vehicles. Coordinates maintenance across family members. Wants one overview, not per-car mental load.*

### Job 4: See all vehicles in one place

> **When** I'm planning the week or month for our household, **I want to** see maintenance status across all our cars at once, **so I can** schedule garage visits efficiently and avoid surprises.

Managing multiple cars multiplies the forgetting problem. A family with 2 cars has 32+ recurring tasks to track.

**Struggling moments:**

- Realizing both cars need tire changes the same week and the garage is booked
- Forgetting which car had the last oil change — was it the Corsa or the Astra?
- Partner saying "I thought you handled the inspection" when neither did

### Job 5: Share car responsibilities with my partner

> **When** my partner and I both use the same car, **I want to** give them access to the maintenance status and let them mark things done too, **so I can** stop being the single point of failure for car maintenance.

**Struggling moments:**

- Texting "did you check the tire pressure?" every month
- Partner not knowing the car is overdue for inspection because only one person tracks it
- Coming home to find the car was driven 200 km with an overdue oil change

## Der Gelegenheitsnutzer (Casual Tech User)

*Not a power user. Installs apps reluctantly. Wants something that works immediately without signup or configuration.*

### Job 6: Start using the app without friction

> **When** someone recommends a car maintenance app, **I want to** open it and see useful content immediately, **so I can** decide if it's worth keeping without investing time in setup.

**Struggling moments:**

- Opening an app and being greeted with a login screen before seeing anything
- Spending 10 minutes configuring settings before the app does anything
- Uninstalling an app because it asked for permissions that felt invasive

### Job 7: Access my car stuff while driving

> **When** I'm in the car and need to navigate somewhere or play music, **I want to** tap once and get to my destination or playlist, **so I can** keep my eyes on the road instead of fumbling with multiple apps.

**Struggling moments:**

- Unlocking the phone, opening Google Maps, typing the mechanic's address while parked
- Searching for "my driving playlist" in Spotify every single time
- Having 3 navigation apps and never remembering which one has the saved location

## Cross-Cutting Jobs

These apply to multiple personas simultaneously.

### Job 8: Not lose my data

> **When** I switch phones, clear my browser, or something goes wrong, **I want to** know my maintenance history is safe, **so I can** pick up where I left off without re-entering everything.
